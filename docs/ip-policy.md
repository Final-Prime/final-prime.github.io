# Final Prime IP policy

Status: current founder-owned policy.

## 1. Current ownership

Final Prime is currently an independent initiative in formation. Original
Final Prime materials are owned by Daniel Kenessy unless a file, contract, or
notice identifies another owner.

No company ownership is implied. Any future transfer to a company must use a
written assignment that identifies the transferred assets and effective date.

## 2. Asset classes

### Copyright assets

Examples include:

- source code;
- website copy and documentation;
- graphics, logos, layouts, diagrams, and audiovisual work;
- reviews, essays, scripts, and editorial structures;
- schemas, tests, examples, and release records; and
- original training, evaluation, and research materials where copyright
  applies.

Copyright protects original expression. It does not by itself protect an
abstract idea, procedure, method of operation, or mathematical concept.

### Trademark assets

Marks are governed by TRADEMARKS.md. New product, project, package, domain,
logo, or service names require clearance before important public or commercial
use.

### Patent-sensitive inventions

A technical disclosure must be classified before publication as one of:

1. patent candidate;
2. trade secret;
3. intentionally disclosed defensive publication; or
4. non-protectable or routine public information.

Public disclosure can affect patent novelty. Patent-sensitive material must
remain private until a filing decision has been made.

### Trade secrets and confidential material

Private source, implementation detail, unreleased research, client records,
security information, private datasets, credentials, commercial terms, and
invention details stay private unless a specific written release decision
approves publication.

## 3. Publication gate

Before public release, record:

- asset owner;
- authors and contributors;
- third-party inputs and licenses;
- mark and title clearance;
- public claim and evidence;
- patent or trade-secret decision;
- privacy and security review;
- public and private boundary;
- release date, commit, and artifact hash; and
- approval by the current owner.

## 4. Third-party material

Do not import code, images, fonts, music, datasets, model outputs, game assets,
screenshots, text, or other material without recording source, owner,
permission, applicable license, and required attribution.

Third-party marks must be used only for truthful identification and must not
imply endorsement or affiliation.

## 5. Contributors and contractors

No outside contribution should be merged before written terms establish:

- the right to provide the work;
- ownership or license scope;
- treatment of pre-existing material;
- confidentiality;
- warranties and provenance;
- attribution, if any; and
- the right to transfer the acquired rights to a future company.

## 6. Evidence and recordkeeping

Keep a private evidence vault containing original source files, dated exports,
design drafts, contracts, assignments, release screenshots, domain records,
repository history, artifact hashes, and first-use evidence.

The public repository may contain a summary register, but sensitive evidence
and personal records must remain off-repository in controlled storage.

## 7. Future company transfer

Before company transfer:

1. identify the legal entity and jurisdiction;
2. inventory copyright, marks, domains, repositories, accounts, contracts,
   and confidential know-how;
3. execute signed assignments and any required contributor consents;
4. update registries, accounts, licenses, notices, and invoices;
5. preserve the chain of title and effective dates; and
6. keep personal rights that are not expressly assigned.

## 8. Enforcement posture

Use clear notices, repository history, private evidence, mark monitoring, and
proportionate written enforcement. Registration and professional advice
should be obtained before high-value filings, disputes, licensing, or company
transfer.
