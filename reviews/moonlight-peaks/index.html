01 / Review contract

Do not score the fantasy before the routine survives.

This page begins as a transparent research surface. It becomes a buyer verdict only after repeated play supports one.

Central test

Fantasy vs routine

Does the vampire identity remain mechanically meaningful after the first-night novelty becomes a repeated farming, social and collection schedule?

Integrity rule

Evidence before collapse

No public number, grade or buy action appears while the core loop, late progression and completion tail remain unknown.

History rule

Append, never rewrite

Early reactions stay visible. Later sessions may challenge them, but the change itself becomes evidence instead of being silently erased.

Current public statement

The creator and opening establish a coherent cozy-gothic identity. The farming, magic, relationship and completion loops remain untested, so quality, buyer action and score stay unknown.

01A / First contact

A polished identity before the first chore begins.

One pre-play baseline and the opening departure are now captured. This is evidence about presentation, tone and premise, not the repeatable life-sim loop.

MP-S00 / Creator

Customization feels authored, not disposable.

Lively animation, clear category icons, useful randomization, strong hairstyle and clothing previews, broad colour control and layered accessories create a distinct starting character. A random-name control is the only current quality-of-life suggestion.

MP-S01 / Departure

Leaving home gives the reset a motive.

The opening moves from reported conflict at a large castle to a train conversation with Mom. Response choices cover conciliatory, definitive and comedic tones before the journey to Moonlight Peaks.

MP-S01 / Arrival

The strange cat is a speaking companion.

The three-eyed figure travels with Daniel, appears with the displayed name Dög and comments on the overgrown property. Its mechanical role, permanence and relationship system remain unknown.

02 / Game-specific lens

MOONLIT replaces mood scoring with a testable completion model.

The older PLATER card remains available as a final compact export. MOONLIT is the working diagnostic layer that preserves time, evidence and uncertainty.

M / Myth and Identity

Does vampirehood change play?

Tracks whether the supernatural identity affects rules, choices, routines and relationships rather than serving only as attractive framing.

O / Ongoing Loop

Does repetition remain satisfying?

Tracks the durability of farming, magic, collection, travel and social routines after novelty begins to decay.

O / Ownership and Agency

Are schedules actually chosen?

Tracks meaningful decisions in time allocation, upgrades, economy, relationships and the shape of the homestead.

N / Neighbourhood and Narrative

Do people become more than tasks?

Tracks character depth, family identity, story momentum and whether relationship progress becomes emotionally or mechanically distinct.

L / Long-tail Completion

Mastery or volume?

Separates discovery, mastery and authored closure from collection debt, repetition and checklist cleanup.

I / Information and Trust

Can the player reason inside the game?

Tracks rule clarity, quest readability, collection state, gift logic, season knowledge and achievement reliability.

T / Technical Readiness

Can a long run be trusted?

Tracks saves, performance, input, progression triggers, patch behavior and achievement integrity across the playthrough.

PLATER bridge

Compact only after evidence

Project, Loop, Atmosphere, Theme, Essentials and Replay will be derived from the mature evidence board instead of scored from first impressions.

Final Prime crosswalk: MOONLIT remains comparable across the catalogue through the shared Promise, Core Loop, Agency, Trust, Time and Readiness hero axes.

03 / Evidence capture

Messy gameplay input becomes a loss-aware public record.

Gameplay reports do not need to arrive in a formal template. Events, reactions, screenshots, criticism and answers can be compressed without pretending missing details were observed.

  1. Raw gameplay input

    What happened, what felt good or wrong, what was confusing, screenshots, progress, patch and playtime.

  2. Session record

    A stable MP-Sxx identifier, lossless summary, milestone, micro-signals, axis deltas and open questions.

  3. Evidence objects

    Claims, issues, screenshot records, achievement events, relationship threads and patch retests receive separate identifiers.

  4. State update

    Repeated evidence changes axis direction, confidence and the next evidence gate without rewriting old sessions.

  5. Milestone publication

    The public route changes when the evidence state changes, not every time an isolated opinion is recorded.

04 / Written playthrough

The run can become a readable chronicle, not a transcript dump.

The chronicle reconstructs only supplied or directly supported events. It can preserve the evolution of the player's judgment without inventing scenes between screenshots.

05 / Completion observatory

Finishing the game and completing it are separate verdicts.

The catalogue will track what each completion goal asks the player to understand, repeat, discover or endure.

Natural progress

Earned through ordinary play

Mainline, first-use and milestone achievements that emerge from engaging with the intended loop.

Mastery

Knowledge becomes competence

Goals that reward understanding, route planning, system use or deliberate optimization.

Discovery

Exploration with authored payoff

Collections and secrets that create meaningful places, stories or mechanical understanding.

Relationship tail

Depth or repeated gifts

Tracks whether broad social completion produces distinct content or repeats the same transactional loop.

Volume grind

Quantity without new decisions

Flags economic totals, repeated actions and collection cleanup that mainly consume time.

Trust risk

Missable, obscure or unstable

Tracks external-guide dependence, hidden conditions, retroactive triggers, save risk and patch-sensitive progress.

09 / Provenance and limits

Trust the boundary as much as the verdict.

The dossier distinguishes direct play evidence, supplied screenshots, official context, interpretation and areas the available inputs cannot establish.

Evidence base

Player-supplied record

Gameplay events, opinions, criticism, answers, playtime, build notes, Steam progress and screenshots supplied during the run.

Append rule

History is not rewritten

Old observations remain intact. Later evidence can challenge them, but the change stays visible.

Research boundary

Context is not experience

Store claims, patch notes and public achievement data guide questions. They do not prove how the reviewed run behaves.

Media boundary

No invented motion or sound

Still images and written descriptions cannot fully establish animation, audio balance, frame pacing or continuous performance.

Narration boundary

Script is the durable artifact

Narration-ready copy and cue sheets are reliable. Device-dependent browser speech is not an authored custom voice track.
